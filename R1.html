All the informations we need are the name price, sales and comments of all the clothes.

From the picture, a lot of clothes fall into the range of 200RMB-400RMB, fewer cost more than 650RMB and none cost 400RMB-650RMB.

Then in order to figure out the sales of clothes falling into each range, we need to recode the price at first and add “pricelevel” variable after that. 

Modadress<-within(Modadres,{
pricelevel<-"NA"
pricelevel[dressprice<450]<-"normal"
pricelevel[dressprice>500]<-"high"
})

Calculate the sales of clothes falling into each range.

total<-tapply(Modadress$salenumber,Modadress$pricelevel,sum)

Step 4: Get the data we what and rule out others

At first we need to specify the meaning of “Popolar clothers”. The standard I refer to is comment volume and sales volume.

According to the figure above, the lighter the color of the blue dot, the larger the comment volume.

Set 1500 as dividing line and choose the best-selling products.

filter(Modadress,thecomment>1500)%>%arrange(desc(salenumber))
